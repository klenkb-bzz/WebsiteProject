1998:
The Chinese company was founded in 1998 and a short time later, Tencent's chat app OICQ came onto the Chinese market. This was an almost one-to-one clone of ICQ. Since the name was too similar to the original, they renamed it QQ.

2004:
This year, Tencent began copying third-party video games and introducing them to the Chinese market. These were mostly mobile games. As a result, Tencent quickly grew into China's leading game publisher. The Chinese leadership has now also noticed about Tencent and is now injecting employees into Tencent in order to control the company.


2008:
In 2008, Tencent acquired 22.34% of the shares in Riot Games, a well-known company in the video game industry that achieved a large increase in players with the game “League of Legends”.

2011:
In 2011, Tencent increased its stake in Riot Games to a whopping 92.78%. At this point, Riot Games was one of the most well-known game developers in the video game market.


2012:
Tencent bought 48.4% of the shares of a small gaming company called Epic Games in 2012. Today it is one of the most successful game developers with their top hit “Fortnite”, which was released in 2017 and quickly gained popularity.

2016:
For $8.6 billion, Tencent buys 84.3% of Supercell, which published many smartphone games in Western countries, such as “Clash of Clans” & “Clash Royal,” all of which made large profits.


2017:
The development studio Bluehole, which is behind the popular game “PUBG”, was banned from the Chinese market by Tencent in 2017. The reason for this is that the game would have too many bloodthirsty scenes. A short time later, the next game clone from Tencent appeared. “Game of Peace” has replaced PUBG on the Chinese market. The game was copied entirely, with the bloody scenes removed and Chinese propaganda added. Even the player data from PUBG has been transferred to the new game.



2019:
In 2019, China asked Tencent to install an identification system in all games that Tencent published. One way to achieve this is to use your full name and install facial recognition software in certain games. The software is intended to limit children's freedom. Now the state only allows children to play video games for 90 minutes a day, up to 3 hours on public holidays and not at all between 10 p.m. and 8 a.m.
According to a statement by Prof. Dr. Sebastian Heilmann, who is one of Europe's most prominent China researchers, calls China a "techno-autocracy". “China now has a new form of government, market supervision and control,” he continued. Later, there was an investigation by Amnesty International that gave Tencent's WeChat app (a WhatsApp clone with features like bank transfers and searching the censored internet) a score of 0/100 in a privacy ranking because the Chinese The government receives virtually all of the data from this app and can do whatever they want with it.
Tencent has now become the global market leader in the gaming industry and now has the upper hand over many major companies.
