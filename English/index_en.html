Overview
Tencent, a Chinese public company headquartered in Shenzhen, China, occupies an important place in the economy. Under the leadership of CEO Ma Huateng and President Martin Lau, Tencent recorded an impressive revenue of 70.6 billion euros in 2022. This impressive achievement places the company as the seventh largest company in the world.

The steady increase in the number of employees, which already stood at 108,436 in 2022, is helping to make Tencent potentially the largest company in the world. This expansion not only reflects economic success, but also highlights Tencent's global importance and influence in various sectors.
Topic
This website is all about the Tencent monopoly. How it grew from a small company that copied video games for the Chinese smartphone market to the billion-dollar company that owns a majority of shares in the most popular gaming companies. It also discusses how the People's Republic of China uses Tencent as a control body over its own people. This power is expanding not only in China, but also in Western countries. There are increasing cases of propaganda and censorship that are directly and indirectly linked to Tencent and the People's Republic of China. These developments shed light on Tencent's global impact and the potential consequences for freedom of expression and information in the digital era.
